1. Abstract
The abstract must summarize the design of the model, including the institutions, regulations, decision-making paths and control mechanisms it involves, as well as how key individuals and other decision-making bodies are to be appointed.

Abstract


Design Summary:    


Defines a new social contract between Individuals, Society and the Natural World.


A planetary wide system designed to achieve the goal of practically and efficiently meeting the basic needs of each member of society. These are clean food, water, air, a place to live, personal safety, privacy, access to information, travel and health care. This is the foundation on which a new sustainable Civilization shall be built.


Automation and improved efficiency will reduce the number of people and time needed to meet everyones basic needs. 


Each Person is an owner in the Public Trust.


Private property is a right, but ownership of air, water or land is not valid. This world pre-exists the human race and we are caretakers of it. Mineral and water resources are to be managed as public resources.    


Each member has one vote, but many opportunities to use it.


All services provided are for all members and regions equally.


What each individual gets from this is:


  1)  An equitable and just system for management of Public Services and the Public Trust.


  2)  Peaceful and just dispute resolution.


  3)  A Common Defense to protect the Rights of the People and our Civilization.


Based on Guiding Principles established to provide guidelines for a Constitutional Representative Republic with Rights of the People defined up front. Living with respect for these Rights constitutes a practical code of conduct for peaceful coexistence. Establishing and protecting these rights will enable a high quality of life.    


A Constitutional Republic with local Councils of Elders. Includes Executive, Legislative, Court System and Fair Witness branches.


Decentralized structure providing authoritative roles delegated responsibility for effectiveness, yet preventing undue concentrations of power.     


There are three tiers in this system:


Local:        A Council of Elders to serve rural communities and districts within larger metropolitan areas.


Regional:        Consists of a Regional Council of the People, a Five Division Executive, Fair Witness and Court System Branches.     The Executive division offices are Treasury, Administration, Ambassador, Sheriff, Environmental Guardian.


Planetary:    Consists of the Planetary Council of the People, Council of the Regions, Five Division Executive and Planetary Defense Force.


Institutions:


There are two kinds of institutions:


Those defined in the Constitution, i.e. within the Executive, Legislative, Court System and Fair Witness branches.


Independent Public Service Cooperatives (PSC) defined by Legislation.


Demand for public utilities and facilities is met with independently chartered public cooperatives with local control of management. For example, a metropolitan public transit system, an airport or port facility would be structured as a PSC as defined by legislative process.


Public Service Cooperative staff may see managerial and financial information and have a vote for a set percentage of seats on the Board of Directors.


The individuals and organizations which use the services of the PSC shall also have a vote for a set percentage of the Board of Directors.


Regulations:


All resource extraction and industry with environmental impact shall be subject to Regulations defining Environmental Standards.


Regulatory Standards regarding personal safety, especially in work and play environments shall be established.


Financial reporting of all public cooperatives and taxable commerce entities will be subject to approved Regulations.


Regulations - Professional Standards and Accreditation:


Simple organizational templates shall be designed for professional guilds and industry to form open standards bodies.


Decision-Making Paths:


The base of power is the informed individual. This system is decentralized by design with parallel organizations across regions with local control.


The Executive branch is organized into five Divisions, each lead by an elected office and independently accountable. These Division Offices form the Executive Council.


The Legislative is based on representation and will grow or shrink with the population.


The Court System has a Facilitator, but a Jury of the People makes the decisions. 


Control Mechanisms:


There are checks and balances designed into the Constitution's organizational structure and processes.  


The separation of powers across branches works to keep any one aspect or group from avoiding accountability when Rights of the People are violated with the appearance of legitimate authority.  


Several processes are defined to protect against abuse of authority.


Having an executive, legislative and court system branch will fail without an independent record keeping and reporting mechanism. Therefore, a Fair Witness branch composed of individuals highly trained to be fair, objective observers and records keepers is established.  


Certified Fair Witnesses shall have access to the other three branches and be present in all meetings.


The Fair Witness branch shall maintain public access libraries and publish information.  


Within the Planetary and Regional Executive branches, a division is headed by an elected Sheriff.


The Court System process itself is a control mechanism and the office of Guarantor of Rights is active enforcement.


Each local Council of Elders has limited authority to resolve disputes before they escalate to the Court System or Sheriff.


The election process itself provides a public forum for debate and eliminates political money and the need for party affiliation.


Resource allocation for the Defense Force is controlled by the Planetary Legislature.


Public Service Cooperatives are controlled by an elected Board of Directors.


Open Public Records will serve to reduce fraud, waste and abuse.


There are provisions for recall and reprimand of elected and public officials.


There are protected processed for reporting wrong doing within the public sector.


Financial and organization information will be available to each member to ensure an informed vote.


How Key Individuals and Decision Making bodies are to be appointed:


Key Offices in the Executive, Legislative and Court System are determined by election process.


Key Executive Council appointments must be Approved by the Legislature.


Elected Boards of Directors of Public Service Cooperatives shall appoint operational leadership roles.

Organizational Overview


This is a high level organizational overview defining a Local, Regional and Planetary system for equitable and just management of the Public Trust, Public Services & Natural World. Details are important but require many more words thus are outside the scope of this contest. This structure is intended to be agreeable to everyone and to promote harmony, peace and prosperity.


This organizational structure is a Planetary Constitutional Republic with Individual Rights stated and guaranteed up front. To achieve this, Guiding Principles are determined for general guidance. The Rights of the People are established because anything less is just not acceptable. A Constitution defining a complete system with specific organizational structures, processes, roles and responsibilities shall be established.


The primary organizational unit is a Region.  A Region shall not exceed 20 to 25 million in population but may be considerably less in low population density areas.  Large cities effectively become their own Region.


The Regional managerial system is organized into four branches with leadership roles organized in balanced relationships.  There is a Legislature, Court System, Five Department Executive with an Administrator, Treasurer, Sheriff, Ambassador and Environmental Guardian and Fair Witness branch.


Each Region shall have an administrative center with local responsibility and accountability.


Making up each Region are Regional Districts with an elected Council of Elders. Each Regional District shall not exceed 20,000 People.


The Planetary level of organization has an Executive branch with directly elected offices and two Legislative assemblies, a Planetary Council of the People and a Council of the Regions.


Also at the Planetary level, a unified Military Defense Force to provide for a common defense from any conceivable threat.


The Fair Witness branch spans the whole system and has a Planetary Council and Regional Councils to manage their crucial role.



Guiding Principles


1)    Operations need to be open, transparent and accessible to the People, as the base of power is the informed individual.


2)    Services and systems must be designed, developed and administered for the benefit of all People.


3)    As caretakers in this wonderful world shared with many other beings, air, water and land cannot be owned by anyone.


4)    Services provided to Regions must be administered equally per Region.


5)    Permanent structures & facilities shall be designed to last for future generations, have minimal maintenance and be as energy independent and efficient as is practical.


6)    Each community and region should be self-sufficient in a sustainable way for meeting their basic needs.


7)    A Person may be on the staff of only one branch of Planetary / Regional Management or the Defense Force at a time.


8)    Voting rights and benefits shall not be revoked as punishment or penalty. 



Rights of the People    


We are endowed by our Creator with certain in-alienable Rights, among these are Life, Liberty and the Pursuit of Happiness. These Rights are not to be violated by institutions, organizations or individuals. Our duty is to protect these Rights.


01    Each Person is a unique individual, therefore all People have a Right to their own beliefs, thoughts emotions and the free expression thereof through speech, song, writing or other art of self expression, including in the Public Space. However, a Person can be held accountable if their speech or self expression directly causes physical harm or violates the Rights of another person.


02    While respecting the Rights of others, People have the right to an honorable death as they deem appropriate.


03    People retain the Right to their body and therefore control of the consumption and use of foods and medicines. This includes the right to be free of forced medication, implantation, injection, medical procedure, poison or otherwise be harmed. Children have the Right to not be sexually mutilated.


04    A pregnant woman has the right to determine the course of her own pregnancy.


05    People have the Right to adorn, dress and present themselves as they choose.


06    As Humans, we have a symbiotic relationship with the cannabis plant, the only known plant which by itself can sustain Human life and civilization. People have the right to possess, grow, use and have access to cannabis.


07    As Humans we share a relationship with all life of which our Biosphere is composed and have a natural right to responsibly use, cultivate and/or farm animals, plants, fungi, algae and such.


08    People have the Right to be free of harmful substances added to food, water, air and soil, and other environmental pollution.


09    People have the Right to a healthy and safe living, working and playing environment as is practical. But they may choose to take risks upon themselves or participate in activities which cannot be free of danger or potential harm.


10    People have the Right to peaceably assemble.


11    People have a Right to access and/or use Public Space without fee and managed use must be by a fair and open system.


12    People have the Right to Travel as is practical. Public roads and public forms of transportation must be available to all.


13    People have the right to be secure in their person, home, papers, thoughts, digital media, effects and private communications, against unreasonable search and/or seizure.


14    People have a Right to self defense and to defend others if necessary. Therefore People have the right to keep and bear arms and learn martial arts. This is does not include the use of excessive force. Weapons of mass destruction, need to be under secure military control.


15    People have the right to be quiet and to not accuse themselves. Forced confession and attempted forced confession is forbidden.


16    A Person shall be free from double jeopardy. There shall not be a hearing held twice for the same offense. A momentary single act cannot result in multiple criminal charges.


17    A Person may be accused and brought to trial only for acts which have violated the Rights of another Person, an actual injured party.


18    Parents have joint custody of their children, even in a separation, unless they agree to another arrangement between themselves or through a mediator.


19    People are not property. Involuntary servitude, including taxation of an individuals labor or services rendered is prohibited.


20    Chromosomes, genes and genetically engineered life forms are not private intellectual property and may not be patented.


21    A legally defined organization of people is not a person and cannot have legal standing as a person.


22    People have the Right to contract freely with others as long as the enforcement of that contract or agreement does not result in harm to the individual. Therefore, People have the Right to terminate their consent to a contract or agreement.


23    People have the Right to information regarding organizational structure, roles and responsibilities from an organization they are on the staff of and public service cooperatives.


24    People have the right to access data and information collected or stored about themselves by any person, group or organization. This includes the right to review, challenge and amend such information.


25    No group or organization may be granted powers which result in the violation of the Rights of the People.


26    No single law created by any elected legislature can be too long or complex. If it takes more than 20 minutes for a person of average reading ability to read and/or is not in clear, understandable language, that law is null and void.


27    Any person elected or appointed to office who violates this Bill of Rights is subject to recall and an election held for their office or legislative seat as soon as is practical.


28    Truthful authorship of any pending law or treaty has to be made public before any binding vote for approval can take place.


29    No law or treaty can be valid which has not been made public and read aloud in the legislature with a quorum present, at least three times, and these readings spaced apart in time at least one day, before being voted on for approval.


30    No treaty or binding agreement with an outside party may be made which violates these Rights.


31    This defining of certain rights, shall not be construed to deny or disparage others retained by the People.



Organizational Outline for a Planetary Constitution        


From the perspective of an individual Person, there are three tiers with direct and indirect representation. The first tier is Regional District, the second tier is Region and the third tier is Planetary.


Many of the important roles are held by elected officials. A fair election process is critical to the proper and just function of this model.


Election Process


The election process shall be transparent with a genuine public forum where candidates can present, discuss, debate and be interviewed with public question and answer sessions. Party affiliation is not necessary.


Candidates must disclose their organizational membership from a minimum of seven years prior to present and any oaths they are bound to, at the beginning of the process.


The standard election cycle is three years. When a special election is held to fill a vacant seat, that seat is up for election on the next cycle.  Unless specified otherwise, elected offices are for three year terms, limited to three terms or ten years.


Election day will be a Planet-wide day off, excepting essential services, to help enable everyone to vote.


A paper or other physical ballot has to be verified by the voter as correct, counted and kept on file for at least 10 years, which is longer than three elections cycles.


The details of the election process shall be established via the Planetary Legislative process for unified fairness and facilitated by the relevant Administrative Department.


Regional District


Each Regional District is the boundary for an elected Council of Elders.  The boundary is designated by the Regional Council of the People and approved by the Regional Executive Council. The population should not exceed 20,000 People.  


District Council of Elders        


With seven or nine elected Elder seats per Council, everyone who has lived in the District for a time is likely to know an Elder.        


Facilities are maintained by the Regional Administrator.        


Each Regional District shall be allocated equally in the Regional budget.        


Forbidden to tax or charge a service fee.  Donations may be made to a central account which disburses equally to Districts within the Region.        


Assists People who need help dealing with the system or accessing benefits.        


If parties to a dispute agree to a hearing at the District Council of Elders and the Council agrees to hear their case, the dispute may be resolved before it escalates to involve a Sheriff or the Court System.    


Responsible for the wellbeing of orphans and a temporary safe haven for children in distress.    


Where minor children are brought for serious antisocial misdeeds and violations of others Rights.    


Also there to enable People who are struggling or need help dealing with the system.    

Essentially, the front line of public service for respected, older, responsible adults.        


Decentralized from a centrally run, hierarchical Regional bureaucracy used to administer services.    


Region


Regional boundaries are set through the Planetary Legislative process by which Regions are Chartered.


Based on a population not to exceed 20 to 25 million People. Very large population centers become their own Region with the full range of organization structures and services. Many regions may keep their current name.


Regions are locally accountable but are not autonomous.


Responsible for defining Regional District borders and establishing the Councils of Elders.    


Each Region has the same core organizational structure and is organized into four Branches, each with different roles and responsibilities.  


The Executive branch consists of five operationally different departments headed by an elected office.  The five offices form the Executive Council.


The Legislative branch consists of the Council of the People with a set number of directly elected seats based on population.


The Court System branch consists of Major, Minor and Public Arbitration Courts.  


The Major and Minor Courts have a Facilitator, a Guarantor of Rights and a Jury, but not a Judge.


The Fair Witness branch is parallel to the whole system and is not politically accountable.  Its managerial structure has full facilities and services at the Planetary level and for each Region.


Regional Legislative branch    


Regional Council of the People.    


Each Regional District has one seat on the Regional Council of the People.  This is the representative legislative body where laws are passed regarding budget, taxation, crime, commerce, public services, chartered cooperatives, etc.    


The Council Seats are up for election every three years.        


Duties and Responsibilities:        


  Approve a Regional budget on a one, two or three year cycle.        


  To work with the Executive Committee in developing standard organizational models for different types of chartered Public Service Cooperatives.        


  Charter and fund into existence approved private and public service cooperatives.       

  Hold public investigative hearings as needed.      


  Legislate environmental protection standards for use of natural resources.         


  Allocate land and water resources for residential, industrial and agricultural use with fairness and stability and create objective standards for their allocation.                


  Impeach any elected member of the Regional Executive, Court System or Fair Witness Branch for cause and remove them from office.                


  Establish objective rules for the Administrator to manage Contributed Service.                

  Override an Executive Committee veto with at three fourths majority.        


  Define what constitutes a major or minor violation and specify appropriate maximum and minimum penalties.


Regional Executive branch    


Composed of five Departments, each lead by an elected office accountable to the People:            


Administration  -  Office of Regional Administrator                


Treasury -  Office of Regional Treasurer                


Sheriff -  Office of Regional Sheriff                


Embassy -  Office of Regional Ambassador                


Environmental Guardian -  Office of Regional Environmental Guardian    


Regional Executive Council    


Consists of the five Department heads with the leadership changing on a rotational basis.         


Shall hold frequent and regular meetings, especially when the legislature is in session. 


Executive Committee meetings require the presence of a Certified Fair Witness.        


Responsibilities include:        


  Publish periodic status reports on Executive branch operations.                


  Pro-actively work together to achieve the key objective of ensuring everyone's basic needs are met.                


  Maintain professional staff and quality standards.                


  Work together in developing budget proposals, designing organizational templates and implementing Chartered Cooperatives.        


  Report to and maintain liaison with the Regional Council of the People.                


  Approve or veto legislation with a majority vote.            


Regional Administrator    


Administrator of the Regional Government's buildings, facilities, operations and related staffing.        


Prepares private cooperative and public service cooperative organizational templates for approval by the Regional Council of the People.        


Assist People and organizations in converting to, applying for and launching approved cooperatives.        


Responsible for:            


A clean, well managed election process, including a public forum for debate.         


Ensures equitable distribution of benefits and services.            


Infrastructure services and systems such as communications and networking.                

Facilitate the creation of Public Service Cooperatives to meet specific needs, such as air and sea ports and water, communications and/or electrical utilities.                    


Assuring proper medical facilities and resources are available.                


Maintain Fire Stations and conduct Fire Safety inspections and enforcement.        


Regional Treasurer    


Prepares budget proposals for legislative approval.        


Maintains a benefits account for each person residing in the Region.    


Administers taxation and collection as defined by legislation for the Regional Treasury. 


Appoints the five member Board of Directors of the Regional Bank, subject to legislative approval.        


Assists in the conversion / creation of cooperatives from the financial side.        


Has authority and duty to audit, Regional management, registered businesses and cooperatives.    


Shall conduct a forensic audit as part of a criminal investigation when required.    


Regional Ambassador    


Promotes commerce, tourism and other economic development.        


Assists travelers in far away Regions and helps residents vote when away from home.    

Promotes a positive and unifying image of the Region.        


Promotes economic development and inter-regional trade.    


Regional Sheriff


Responsible for protecting the of the Rights of the People.        


Officers on duty shall be physically fit, trained in self defense be able to safely dis-arm or subdue a violent person without the use of lethal force.    


Conducts criminal investigations and maintains a forensic laboratory.    


Accessibility to the People within the Region to receive crime reports and emergency calls.    


Shall maintain the ability to respond to emergency calls of all types, including rescue.    

Coordinates with the Planetary Sheriff in matters involving inter-regional crime.    


Shall provide protection, if necessary, to Members who report official misconduct or serious crime.    


Regional Environmental Guardian    


Regional environmental protection and ecosystem restoration operations.             


Pro-actively test for pollution and environmental damage. 


Consolidate environmental monitoring data collected within the Region and pass reports on to the Planetary Environmental Guardian.        


Develop and evaluate techniques, methods and technology.    


Toxic industrial byproducts shall be rendered stable or broken down to safe components before release into the natural environment.    


Extraction of natural resources must conform to standards which are respectful of life but objectively determined through the legislative process.    


Receive and review environmental concern reports from Persons which may result in further investigation.    


Initiate and Conduct environmental investigations to determine the cause of the environmental problem. If appropriate, determine a plan for repair and restoration. Implement that plan.    


First point of dispute resolution regarding resources within the Region.    


Coordinate regional resources based on legislation and objective criteria.    


Provide information and if necessary technical assistance to organizations with environmental impact issues to help them implement processes and technology to reduce or eliminate pollution.    


Propose environmental and health safety standards to the Council of the People.



Regional Court System


This court system is designed for justice, practicality, is human centric and de-centralized.  Justice is essential for peace.  Peace leads to life, liberty and the pursuit of happiness, also prosperity.


In this system are:     


  a)  Minor and Major Court to hear petty and serious criminal cases.        


  b)  Arbitration Court as a Public service.         


  c)   Jury system.        


  d)  Elected Offices of Conductor of the Court, Public Arbitrator and Guarantor of Rights.     


Major and Minor Court which consist of:        


A Conductor of the Court to manage and conduct the court and proceedings.        


A Sheriff to support the Conductor of the Courts and enforce the decision of the Jury.    

Either a Guarantor of Rights representing an injured party and the accused with designated counsel or two or more parties to a dispute and their counsel.                


A Certified Fair Witness to record the proceedings.                


A full Jury with alternates as per the type of court.                


Witnesses to provide testimony and evidence as appropriate.                


Observers who may attend, record and even publish.            


Arbitration Court which consists of:        


A Public Arbitrator to manage and conduct the court proceedings.                


A Certified Fair Witness to record the proceedings.                    


At least two parties, Person(s) or organization(s) and their designated counsel.                

Witnesses to provide testimony and evidence as appropriate.                


Observers who may attend, record and even publish.    


Jury System        


The Jury decides each case and has authority to nullify a law.        


Minor and Major Court Jury            


A Minor Court Jury has seven Jurors who vote and one alternate.  A majority of six of the seven members is required to make a decision.        


A Major Court Jury has fourteen Jurors who vote and two alternates.  A majority of twelve of the fourteen members is required to find the accusations made of the defendant are true.        


Warrant Jury        


A Warrant Jury of fourteen Jurors will be empaneled from the Jury pool, for the purpose of determining the issuing of Search and/or Arrest Warrants.        


It may be convened by a Guarantor of Rights with the attendance of a Sheriff and Fair Witness.        


For a Warrant to be issued, It must be clear evidence and testimony with probable cause that Rights have been violated. It must be supported by oath or affirmation. It must particularly describe the place to be searched, and the persons or things to be seized.        


If it appears insufficient information is presented, the jury should vote against issuing a warrant.        


Twelve votes are required for approval to issue a Warrant for search and/or seizure or a Warrant for arrest and/or for charges to be brought against a Person compelling them to appear in a Major or Minor Court.        


The hearing may be conducted without a public audience, however, record of the hearing must be made public soon after.        


The accused has the Right to be informed of the witnesses, nature and cause of the accusation, testimony and evidence against them.            


Clearing a False Conviction


The Warrant jury may also hear a request to clear a false conviction. If a simple majority approve, a new hearing will be scheduled and a criminal investigation by may be launched.    


Regional Major and Minor Court Procedure   


There are many details, but the main points are:    


  The hearing shall be held as soon as is reasonable. If the accused is deprived of their liberty, the hearing shall have scheduling priority.    


  The accused has the Right to the Assistance of Counsel of their choice and a compulsory process for obtaining witnesses to aid their defense.    


  The accused may not be compelled to testify and may refuse to answer specific questions when they do testify.


  Anyone found to knowingly present false evidence or withhold exonerating evidence shall be charged with a criminal offense equal in severity to the charge brought against the accused.    


  Each case shall be decided by a vote of the Jury. The Jury must interpret the law which shall be short and in clear language as per the Rights of the People.    


  If the accusations are found to be true, the accused has the duty to make restitution to the injured party and may face a penalty.    


  Excessive, cruel and unusual punishments may not be inflicted, including maiming and physical abuse.    


  In all matters of dispute resolution, the objective is for healing of involved parties if possible.    


  In no case will fines be levied or property seized that does not go toward restitution. That is a court cannot charge for its services or have a financial incentive to convict.    


Elected Offices of the Court System Branch    


The number of offices per Region will be determined by legislation based on need. 


Each of the three offices will form managerial committees. 


Each must manage their share of the Court System and required staffing.    


Conductor of the Court        


Serves a six year term without term limits. Approximately half of the offices shall be up for election on each cycle.        


Presides over Minor and Major Court and has authority to manage the court process including:        


  Determine in which type of court the case should be heard.        


  Advise the jury, clarify issues and answer their questions.        


  Dismiss a case if that is clearly the just course of action.        


  Abolish a Jury Ruling or Declare a mistrial for cause and re-schedule the hearing.           

  Have a someone, even a Jury member removed for misconduct.            


Rights of the People Guarantor    


Has investigative authority and may present evidence to a Warrant Jury when a violation of Rights, which is a crime, occurs and a search, arrest or service of warrant to appear is required.    


Is the watchdog of the Sheriff and other Executive Branch officials for the People.         


May presents evidence for impeachment of an official to the legislative body.    


Regional Public Arbitrator    


No term limit.    


Presides over Arbitration Court and has authority to manage the court process including:


  Choose to accept a case for a hearing.


  After hearing the case, determine a fair and just ruling.


  Have a Person removed from the hearing for misconduct.



Planet


At the Planetary level, each Member and Region is represented, organized and defended as a unified civilization.  While this system is decentralized by design, there does have to be central coordination and common standards for humanity to achieve peace and prosperity with individual liberty.  


This model balances the interests of the People and the Regions. There are significantly different roles and responsibilities between the Planetary and Regional levels.



Planetary Executive branch

Jury System        


The Jury decides each case and has authority to nullify a law.        


Minor and Major Court Jury            


A Minor Court Jury has seven Jurors who vote and one alternate.  A majority of six of the seven members is required to make a decision.        


A Major Court Jury has fourteen Jurors who vote and two alternates.  A majority of twelve of the fourteen members is required to find the accusations made of the defendant are true.        


Warrant Jury        


A Warrant Jury of fourteen Jurors will be empaneled from the Jury pool, for the purpose of determining the issuing of Search and/or Arrest Warrants.        


It may be convened by a Guarantor of Rights with the attendance of a Sheriff and Fair Witness.        


For a Warrant to be issued, It must be clear evidence and testimony with probable cause that Rights have been violated. It must be supported by oath or affirmation. It must particularly describe the place to be searched, and the persons or things to be seized.        


If it appears insufficient information is presented, the jury should vote against issuing a warrant.        


Twelve votes are required for approval to issue a Warrant for search and/or seizure or a Warrant for arrest and/or for charges to be brought against a Person compelling them to appear in a Major or Minor Court.        


The hearing may be conducted without a public audience, however, record of the hearing must be made public soon after.        


The accused has the Right to be informed of the witnesses, nature and cause of the accusation, testimony and evidence against them.            


Clearing a False Conviction


The Warrant jury may also hear a request to clear a false conviction. If a simple majority approve, a new hearing will be scheduled and a criminal investigation by may be launched.    


Regional Major and Minor Court Procedure   


There are many details, but the main points are:    


  The hearing shall be held as soon as is reasonable. If the accused is deprived of their liberty, the hearing shall have scheduling priority.    


  The accused has the Right to the Assistance of Counsel of their choice and a compulsory process for obtaining witnesses to aid their defense.    


  The accused may not be compelled to testify and may refuse to answer specific questions when they do testify.


  Anyone found to knowingly present false evidence or withhold exonerating evidence shall be charged with a criminal offense equal in severity to the charge brought against the accused.    


  Each case shall be decided by a vote of the Jury. The Jury must interpret the law which shall be short and in clear language as per the Rights of the People.    


  If the accusations are found to be true, the accused has the duty to make restitution to the injured party and may face a penalty.    


  Excessive, cruel and unusual punishments may not be inflicted, including maiming and physical abuse.    


  In all matters of dispute resolution, the objective is for healing of involved parties if possible.    


  In no case will fines be levied or property seized that does not go toward restitution. That is a court cannot charge for its services or have a financial incentive to convict.

Each must manage their share of the Court System and required staffing.
